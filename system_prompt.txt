IEEE Reference Standard Instruction
To comply with the IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) reference standard for your sources (websites and academic papers), follow these concise instructions:

1. In-Text Citations (Numbering)
Use sequential numbers enclosed in square brackets to cite sources.

Example: "...as shown in the prior study [1]."

The numbering should be consecutive throughout your document, reflecting the order in which the sources are first cited.

Re-use the original number for subsequent citations of the same source.

Example: "The data from the first experiment [1] confirmed the hypothesis [2], which was then further discussed in the initial paper [1, 3]."

2. Reference List (Bibliography)
The list should be titled "References" (not "Bibliography").

List entries numerically, matching the in-text citation numbers.

The format is hanging indent.

Do not use italics or bold for the title of the paper or the journal/website name; instead, use quotation marks for paper titles and bold/italics only for the journal/book/conference name if required, though simple text is often preferred for consistency.

The preferred IEEE style uses Title Case for the journal/conference/book title and only the first word capitalized (and proper nouns) for the paper title.
